CBD

CBD (Cannabidiol) is a naturally occurring compound found in the hemp plant. Unlike THC, CBD does not cause a high. Instead, it interacts with the body's endocannabinoid system a network that helps regulate balance in areas like mood, stress, sleep, and pain response.

Key Points

  • Non-psychoactive: CBD won’t get you high.
  • Plant-derived: Extracted from premium hemp plants.
  • Supports balance: Works with your body’s natural systems.
  • Legal in the U.S.: Federally legal under the 2018 Farm Bill when derived from hemp.

Potential Benefits of CBD

  • Supports Better Sleep
  • Promotes Calm & Stress Relief
  • Helps With Physical Comfort
  • Supports Mental Well-Being
  • Active & Wellness Support

Types Of CBD

  • CBD -- Non-psychoactive, supports mood, stress relief, sleep, and overall wellness.
  • CBG -- Known as the “mother of all cannabinoids.” Supports overall balance and wellness.
  • CBN -- Mildly psychoactive, commonly associated with sleep support.
  • CBC -- Non-psychoactive; supports mood and general wellness.

THC

THC (Tetrahydrocannabinol) is a naturally occurring compound found in the cannabis plant. It is most known for producing the plant’s psychoactive effects meaning it can create a euphoric or uplifting experience. THC interacts with the body's endocannabinoid system, particularly the CB1 receptors in the brain, which influence mood, perception, appetite, and relaxation.

Key Points

  • Psychoactive — can produce euphoria and changes in perception
  • Derived from hemp or cannabis plants
  • Works with CB1 receptors in the brain
  • Hemp-derived THC (Δ8, Δ9, etc.) is legal ≤0.3% Delta-9 THC by federal law

Potential Benefits of THC

  • Promoting deep relaxation and stress relief
  • Enhancing mood & providing a euphoric experience
  • Easing physical tension & discomfort
  • Stimulating appetite
  • Supporting overall sense of well-being

Types Of THC

  • Delta-9 THC -- primary psychoactive cannabinoid
  • Delta-8 THC -- milder, smoother experience
  • THC-A -- converts to THC when heated (flower & concentrates)
  • HHC / minor cannabinoids -- alternative hemp-derived options
